Tutor Mode

Getting Started
Welcome to dsFinancial Modelling. This tool follows textbook conventions from Damodaran, McKinsey Valuation, and Ross-Westerfield. Toggle Tutor Mode anytime to see explanations for every cell, formula, and assumption.
Ref: Damodaran — Investment Valuation (3rd ed.) Ch. 2
DCF — Free Cash Flow to Firm
FCFF is the cash flow available to all investors (debt + equity) before financing decisions.
FCFF = EBIT × (1 − t) + D&A − CapEx − ΔWC
We discount FCFF at WACC (not Ke) because it represents the blended cost of all capital.
Ref: McKinsey Valuation (7th ed.) Ch. 5
Terminal Value
Terminal Value typically dominates total Enterprise Value (often 60–80%). Two methods:
Gordon: TV = FCFF(n+1) / (WACC − g)
Exit Multiple: TV = EBITDA(n) × Multiple
Sanity check: WACC must exceed terminal growth rate g.
Ref: Damodaran Ch. 3; McKinsey Ch. 9
WACC — Cost of Capital
WACC is the weighted average of cost of equity and post-tax cost of debt.
WACC = (E/V) × Ke + (D/V) × Kd × (1 − t)
CAPM: Ke = Rf + β × ERP
Ref: Ross, Westerfield, Jaffe — Corporate Finance Ch. 13
India-Specific Defaults
Corporate Tax: 25.17% (new domestic companies under sec 115BAA) or 22% (opted-in regime). Default here is 25.17%.
Risk-free: 10Y G-Sec (~6.9%).
ERP: ~7.2% (Damodaran India estimate).
Index: NIFTY 50 for beta regression.
Ref: RBI, NSE, Damodaran ERP Dataset
LBO — Leveraged Buyout
An LBO models acquiring a company using significant debt, with returns driven by EBITDA growth, multiple expansion, and deleveraging.
IRR: NPV(equity invested, exit proceeds, CFs) = 0
MoM = Exit Equity / Entry Equity
Ref: Rosenbaum & Pearl — Investment Banking Ch. 5
M&A — Accretion/Dilution
A deal is accretive if Pro-Forma EPS > Standalone Acquirer EPS.
PF EPS = (NI_acq + NI_tgt + Synergies − Financing Cost) / (Shares_acq + New Shares)
Goodwill = Purchase Price − Net Identifiable Assets.
Ref: Rosenbaum & Pearl Ch. 6
